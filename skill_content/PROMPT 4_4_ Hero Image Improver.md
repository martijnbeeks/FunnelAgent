## THE PROMPT

I'm uploading the hero image we generated. Based on the research and target market we've been working with, tear this image apart.

Be brutal. Be specific. Tell me exactly why it will or won't make someone's chest tighten with recognition.

## EXAMPLE OUTPUT

Here's what a good analysis looks like:

---

**Image:** Woman looking out window

### 1\. THE SCROLL TEST: 4/10

Generic "sad person" energy. Seen this a thousand times. Nothing specific enough to stop the scroll.

### 2\. THE RECOGNITION TEST: 3/10

Could be anyone sad about anything. No specific joint pain moment visible. No "that's me" trigger.

### 3\. THE EMOTION TEST: 3/10

Mild melancholy. No gut-punch. No chest-tightening. Just... vaguely sad. **Primary Emotion:** Mild sadness **Intensity:** 3/10

### 4\. THE SPECIFICITY TEST: 2/10

This is a CONCEPT ("isolation") not a MOMENT. Where's the 5:47am reality? Where's the hands gripping the armchair? Where's the grandchildren she can't reach?

### 5\. TECHNICAL EXECUTION: 5/10

- Expression: Too posed, not raw enough  
- Eyes: Slightly oversaturated blue  
- Lighting: Flat, doesn't serve emotion  
- Composition: Centered, boring  
- Demographics: Close but looks too healthy

**OVERALL: 17/50 — START OVER**

---

## WHAT'S WRONG

1. No specific MOMENT — just a vague "sad by window" concept  
2. Expression is melancholy, not devastating  
3. No physical evidence of her condition  
4. Could be about anything — divorce, loss, loneliness — not specifically about joint pain stealing her life  
5. Nothing that says "I can't get down on the floor with my grandchildren"

---

## IMPROVED PROMPT

### SCENE

First-person POV from an armchair. We're looking DOWN at the floor where two grandchildren (ages 4-6) are playing with wooden blocks. They're looking UP at camera, arms reaching, faces hopeful. "Come play with us, Grandma."

### DEMOGRAPHIC

The hands visible on armrests belong to a 65-year-old British woman. Age spots, wedding ring, slightly swollen knuckles. These are REAL hands that have lived.

### THE PAIN VISIBLE

Knuckles WHITE from gripping armrests. Not because she's angry — because she's trying to find the strength to push herself up. And can't.

### EXPRESSION

N/A — POV shot. But the TENSION is in the hands. The grip. The frozen moment of wanting to move and being trapped.

### COMPOSITION

- Foreground: Her hands, white-knuckled on armrests  
- Middle: The gap — 3 feet of carpet that might as well be the Grand Canyon  
- Background: Grandchildren on floor, bathed in warm light, reaching up

### LIGHTING

Warm golden afternoon light on the grandchildren. Slightly cooler where her hands are. The warmth literally doesn't reach her.

### THE GUT-PUNCH

The children's reaching arms. The hope in their faces. They don't understand why Grandma can't come play. That innocent confusion is devastating.

### WHAT TO AVOID

- Don't show her face (POV shot)  
- No theatrical hand positions  
- Children should look hopeful, not sad  
- No dark/moody lighting on children — they're in the light, she's not

---

## FINAL VERDICT

**Original:** A generic sad-by-window shot that could be about anything. Zero scroll-stopping power. Zero recognition. Zero conversion.

**What it needs:** A specific, visceral MOMENT that every person with joint pain has lived — being three feet from everything they love and unable to close the gap.

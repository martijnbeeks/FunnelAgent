# 55+ HEALTH & BEAUTY SALES PAGE COPY SOP v1.4

## High-Converting Sales Pages for Adults 55+ in Sophisticated Markets

---

## PURPOSE OF THIS SOP

Generate sales page copy that slots directly into the 55+ HTML template, converting solution-aware prospects (Awareness Level 3-4) by:

- Leading with MECHANISM education (why it works when others fail)  
- Using emotion-first visuals (images do the heavy lifting)  
- Keeping text SHORT (55+ readability \= 2-3 sentences per block)  
- Applying proven DR frameworks (PAS, PASTOR, 4 U's, UMP/UMS, "Reason Why")  
- Building trust through doctor narrative \+ social proof  
- Destroying alternatives with logic and data  
- Creating loss aversion (cost of inaction)  
- Making purchase feel like the only intelligent choice

## CRITICAL RULES FOR 55+ COPY

### LANGUAGE LEVEL: B1 ENGLISH (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

Write at B1 (Intermediate) English level:

- Use simple, common words  
- Short sentences (10-15 words average)  
- No jargon unless explained  
- Active voice, not passive  
- One idea per sentence

**B1 Word Choices:** | ❌ Don't Use | ✅ Use Instead | |-------------|----------------| | Utilize | Use | | Commence | Start | | Facilitate | Help | | Insufficient | Not enough | | Subsequently | Then / After | | Inflammation | Swelling / Pain | | Bioavailable | Easy to absorb | | Formulation | Formula / Mix |

### BOLD TEXT REQUIREMENTS (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

**Target: 15-20% of paragraph text should be bold**

How to calculate:

1. Count total words in paragraph  
2. Multiply by 0.15 and 0.20 to get range  
3. Bold that many words  
4. Example: 50-word paragraph \= bold 8-10 words

**What MUST be bolded:**

| Category | Examples |
| :---- | :---- |
| Key pain points | joint pain, stiffness, morning aches, tired legs |
| Mechanism terms | inflammation, cartilage, absorption, root cause |
| Time periods | 7 days, 90 days, Week 1, Month 2 |
| Statistics | 73%, 47,000+, 22 years |
| Negative outcomes | getting worse, damage, breakdown |
| Specific benefits | move freely, pain-free, full mobility |
| Product differentiation | unlike other supplements, targets the source |

**Sections requiring 15-20% bold:**

- Section 6: Agitation (highlight box \+ text)  
- Section 7: Doctor Story (all paragraphs)  
- Section 8: Mechanism (UMP/UMS boxes)  
- Section 10: Alternatives (failure reasons)  
- Section 15: Guarantee (text)  
- Section 16: FAQ answers

**Sections with NO bold:**

- Section 9: Testimonials (keep natural/authentic)  
- Section 11: Timeline (keep clean)

### LAZY LOADING (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

All images MUST include `loading="lazy"` attribute **EXCEPT the hero image**:

**Why?** Hero image is above the fold — it must load instantly for first impression. All other images can load as user scrolls.

This improves page speed and Core Web Vitals.

## SECTION 1: URGENCY BANNER

### Framework: "Reason Why" (Claude Hopkins)

Never use generic scarcity. Always give a BELIEVABLE reason WHY it's limited.

### Formula:

🔥 \[Current Month\] Sale: Save \[X\]% — Limited Batch: Only \[\#\#\#\] bottles available (\[ingredient\] sourcing limits production)

### Dynamic Month:

The banner should automatically show the current month. In HTML template, this is handled by JavaScript:

const months \= \['January', 'February', ...\];

document.getElementById('current-month').textContent \= months\[new Date().getMonth()\];

### Examples:

🔥 January Sale: Save 50% — Limited Batch: Only 127 bottles available (high-potency Boswellia sourcing limits production)

🔥 March Sale: Save 50% — Limited Batch: Only 243 bottles available (high-quality turmeric sourcing limits production)

🔥 September Sale: Save 50% — Limited Batch: Only 89 bottles available (high-potency curcuminoid sourcing limits production)

### Key Ingredient Sourcing Examples:

| Product Type | Sourcing Limitation |
| :---- | :---- |
| Joint Health | high-potency Boswellia sourcing |
| Brain Health | high-quality Lion's Mane sourcing |
| Heart Health | high-potency CoQ10 sourcing |
| Vision Health | high-quality Lutein sourcing |
| Hair/Beauty | high-grade Biotin sourcing |

### Character Limit: 100-140 characters

### What to Extract from Research:

- Hero ingredient that's expensive/rare to source  
- Standardization percentage (e.g., 95% curcuminoids)  
- Any genuine supply constraints

### Verification Checklist:

- [ ] Contains current month (dynamic)  
- [ ] "Limited Batch" framing (not just "limited time")  
- [ ] Includes discount percentage (50%)  
- [ ] Specific bottle count (under 500 for urgency)  
- [ ] Ingredient-specific sourcing reason  
- [ ] 100-140 characters

## 🔴 CRITICAL REVIEW: The DR Gut-Check (MANDATORY)

**STOP. Before you output ANY CONFIG, you MUST complete this emotional audit.**

This is where good copy becomes MONEY copy. You're not checking grammar — you're checking if this copy will make someone's heart race, their finger twitch toward the buy button, and their logical brain shut up long enough for their emotional brain to say "I NEED this."

---

### REVIEW \#1: THE HEADLINE — Does It Hit the Deepest Desire?

The headline is 80% of your ad. Get this wrong, nothing else matters.

**Forget the rules for a moment.** The rules say "include a number" or "mention demographic." Those are training wheels. The REAL question is:

**Does this headline speak to the \#1 thing keeping your prospect awake at 3am?**

#### The Desire Hierarchy (Pick the DOMINANT one):

**For HEALTH products:**

1. 🔴 **Fear of Death/Decline** — "I don't want to end up like my father"  
2. 🟠 **Loss of Independence** — "I refuse to be a burden"  
3. 🟡 **Physical Suffering** — "I just want the pain to stop"  
4. 🟢 **Missing Out on Life** — "I can't play with my grandkids anymore"

**For BEAUTY products:**

1. 🔴 **Feeling Invisible/Undesired** — "My husband doesn't look at me anymore"  
2. 🟠 **Loss of Identity** — "I don't recognize myself in the mirror"  
3. 🟡 **Social Shame** — "I'm embarrassed to go out"  
4. 🟢 **Aging Anxiety** — "Where did the years go?"

#### Headline Gut-Check Questions:

Ask yourself — and be BRUTALLY honest:

1. **The Screenshot Test:** If someone screenshot this headline and sent it to their spouse, would the spouse say "This is exactly what you've been saying"?  
     
2. **The 3am Test:** Would someone lying awake at 3am, in pain, scared, or frustrated — would they see this headline and think "THAT'S ME"?  
     
3. **The "So What" Test:** Read your headline. Now ask "So what?" If you can't immediately answer with something that triggers emotion, rewrite it.  
     
4. **The Competitor Test:** Could ANY of your competitors use this exact headline? If yes, it's too generic. Kill it.

#### Headline Revision Permission:

**YOU ARE AUTHORIZED TO BREAK THE RULES IF:**

- The research shows a desire MORE powerful than what the "formula" headline captures  
- A simpler, more emotional statement would hit harder  
- The target's actual language (from reviews, forums, complaints) is more powerful than your polished version

**Examples of Rule-Breaking Headlines That WORK:**

| Formula Headline (Safe) | Desire-First Headline (Dangerous but Better) |
| :---- | :---- |
| "73% of Adults 55+ Report Less Joint Pain in 14 Days" | "Finally. Wake Up Without Dreading Your First Step." |
| "New Formula Targets Root Cause of Wrinkles" | "Your Husband Will Look. Again." |
| "Doctor-Developed Hearing Support for Adults 60+" | "Stop Pretending You Heard What They Said." |

The formula headline is GOOD. The desire-first headline is VISCERAL.

**Your choice. But choose CONSCIOUSLY.**

#### Headline Revision Checklist:

Before finalizing your headline, confirm:

- [ ] I identified the \#1 dominant desire from the hierarchy above  
- [ ] My headline speaks to that desire — not a lesser one  
- [ ] I read the headline out loud and felt something  
- [ ] A skeptical 60-year-old would NOT roll their eyes at this  
- [ ] This headline could NOT be used by competitors (it's specific to our mechanism or promise)

**If any box is unchecked → REWRITE before proceeding.**

### REVIEW \#2: THE UMP — Does It Create the "Aha" Moment?

The UMP (Unique Mechanism of Problem) must make the reader think:

"THAT'S why nothing has worked\! It's not my fault — there's a ROOT CAUSE I didn't know about\!"

This is the moment where blame shifts from the customer to the problem itself. If your UMP doesn't create this shift, it's just information — not persuasion.

#### UMP Emotional Criteria:

| Criterion | Test Question | Pass/Fail |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Blame Shift** | Does it absolve the reader of past failure? ("It's not that you didn't try hard enough...") |  |
| **Hidden Enemy** | Does it reveal a villain they didn't know existed? (enzyme, toxin, deficiency, process) |  |
| **Age Trigger** | Does it explain why the problem is WORSE now than when they were younger? |  |
| **Specificity** | Does it name a SPECIFIC mechanism (not vague "toxins" or "inflammation")? |  |
| **Hopelessness Breaker** | Does it imply that knowing this changes everything? |  |

#### UMP Red Flags (Rewrite if present):

❌ "Toxins build up in your body" — Too vague. WHAT toxins? WHERE? ❌ "Your body changes as you age" — No shit. Tell me something I don't know. ❌ "Inflammation causes pain" — Everyone knows this. What's the MECHANISM behind the inflammation? ❌ "Stress affects your health" — Lazy. Non-specific. Unpersuasive.

#### UMP Gold Standard Examples:

✅ **Joint Pain:** "After 50, your body produces 50% LESS of the enzyme that controls inflammation. Without this enzyme, your immune system attacks your own cartilage — like friendly fire that never stops."

✅ **Memory:** "Your brain has a 'cleaning system' that flushes out toxic proteins while you sleep. After 60, this system slows by 40% — leaving debris that blocks the signals between neurons."

✅ **Skin Aging:** "Collagen doesn't just 'decrease' — it gets ATTACKED by an enzyme called MMP-1 that your body produces MORE of after menopause. You're not just losing collagen. You're destroying it."

**Notice the pattern:** Specific mechanism → Age-related trigger → Consequence → Emotional implication (it's not your fault, but it IS getting worse)

---

### REVIEW \#3: THE UMS — Does It Feel Like the OBVIOUS Solution?

The UMS (Unique Mechanism of Solution) must make the reader think:

"Of COURSE that's the answer. Why didn't anyone tell me this before?"

It should feel like the logical, inevitable response to the UMP. If someone reads your UMP and UMS back-to-back, there should be a "click" — like two puzzle pieces fitting.

#### The UMP → UMS Bridge Test:

Write your UMP and UMS as a single sentence using "so" or "which is why":

"\[UMP\], **which is why** \[UMS\]"

If this sentence feels awkward, forced, or requires explanation — the bridge is broken.

**Example (Broken Bridge):**

- UMP: "Inflammation damages your joints after 50"  
- UMS: "FlexiRelief uses 5 clinically-proven ingredients"  
- Bridge: "Inflammation damages your joints after 50, which is why FlexiRelief uses 5 clinically-proven ingredients" ❌ WEAK — doesn't connect

**Example (Strong Bridge):**

- UMP: "After 50, your body produces 50% less of the enzyme that controls inflammation"  
- UMS: "FlexiRelief's Boswellia extract directly inhibits the 5-LOX pathway — the same pathway your body can no longer regulate on its own"  
- Bridge: "After 50, your body produces 50% less of the enzyme that controls inflammation, which is why FlexiRelief's Boswellia extract directly inhibits the 5-LOX pathway — the same pathway your body can no longer regulate." ✅ CLICKS

#### UMS Emotional Criteria:

| Criterion | Test Question | Pass/Fail |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Direct Response** | Does it directly address the ROOT CAUSE from the UMP? |  |
| **Mechanism Clarity** | Can a 55-year-old understand HOW it works in one read? |  |
| **Differentiation** | Does it explain why THIS solution works when others don't? |  |
| **Credibility** | Does it sound scientific but not intimidating? |  |
| **Inevitability** | After reading UMP \+ UMS, does buying feel like the ONLY logical choice? |  |

---

### REVIEW \#4: THE HEADLINE ↔ UMP ↔ UMS ALIGNMENT

All three must tell the SAME story. If your headline promises "pain relief," your UMP better explain why pain exists, and your UMS better explain how the product stops it.

**Alignment Test:**

| Element | Core Promise | Aligned? |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Headline | \[What does it promise?\] |  |
| UMP | \[What problem does it explain?\] |  |
| UMS | \[What solution does it offer?\] |  |

All three should be answering the SAME question from different angles:

- Headline \= "Here's what you'll get"  
- UMP \= "Here's why you don't have it"  
- UMS \= "Here's how we give it to you"

If ANY element is talking about something different — you have a leak in your funnel. Fix it.

---

### REVIEW COMPLETE — Final Gut Check

Before outputting your CONFIG, answer honestly:

1. **Would YOU buy this product based on this copy?** (If you hesitate, your customer will too)  
     
2. **Did you feel anything while writing the headline?** (If you felt nothing, your reader will feel nothing)  
     
3. **Could you explain the UMP/UMS to your mother in 30 seconds?** (If not, it's too complicated)  
     
4. **Is there a single LINE in this copy that would make someone stop scrolling and say "wait, what?"** (You need at least one)

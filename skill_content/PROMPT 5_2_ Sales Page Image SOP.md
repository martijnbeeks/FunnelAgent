# **PART 2: UNIVERSAL STANDARDS**

Apply ALL of these to EVERY image prompt generated by this SOP.

## **Mandatory Product Display Specification**

Add this exact line to EVERY prompt that includes the product:

"Product must exactly replicate the reference image: identical bottle/bag shape,

label design, colors, cap style, brand placement, and overall packaging aesthetic

— no variations or interpretations allowed."

## **Product Size Guidelines**

Never use frame percentages. Use physical size descriptions that AI generators understand:

* In hand: Natural single-hand grip, thumb and middle finger wrap around approximately half the diameter

* On surface: About the height of a large coffee mug

* Product should look natural and proportional — like a real product used daily

* NOT comically large or unusually tiny — match natural hand/surface proportions

## **Color Theme Matching (Add to ALL Prompts)**

* Match the dominant color palette of the uploaded product packaging

* Coordinate backgrounds, accent colors, and overall warmth/coolness with product branding

* Create visual harmony between product colors and image design elements

* Subtle integration — should feel cohesive, not forced

## **Eye Color Realism (CRITICAL)**

**USE:** brown, hazel, grey, green, soft blue, grey-blue, muted blue

**NEVER:** "bright blue," "piercing," "electric," "striking," "vivid," "bright," "intense"

Always specify in prompt: "natural, realistic eye color"

## **Expression Standard**

Customer expression: HAPPY — content, confident. NO surprised or exaggerated expressions. Dignified emotion, not theatrical.

## **Text Rules**

NO TEXT IN ANY IMAGE — EXCEPT:

* Mechanism Diagrams (max 4 labels, max 15 words total, max 5 words per label)

* Alternatives Diagrams (max 4 labels, max 15 words total, max 5 words per label)

* All diagram text in TARGET LANGUAGE

## **Generation Standard**

Generate 2 variations of EVERY image → Pick best. Always generate with 1K maximum resolution.

## **Hands Rule**

Specify hand position in EVERY portrait prompt to avoid AI artifacts. Examples: "hands naturally at sides," "hands in lap," "hands holding mug," "hands engaged in activity."

## **Agitation Philosophy**

This is the "recognition moment" — reader sees themselves. Must be DIGNIFIED struggle, not theatrical suffering. The 55+ audience rejects pity.

**CRITICAL: Every agitation image has TWO LAYERS:**

* Layer 1: The Emotional Moment — setting, body language, private struggle

* Layer 2: The Condition Anchor — a specific visual element tying the emotion to THIS product's specific problem

## **The Condition Anchor (MANDATORY)**

Every agitation prompt MUST include a Condition Anchor. Without it, the image becomes generic "person feeling bad" that could be about anything.

| Anchor Type | What It Is | Use When | Examples |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| BODY FOCUS | Hand/posture/gaze draws attention to affected area | Condition has specific body location | Hand on jaw (gums), gripping knee (joints), touching temples (headache) |
| TRIGGER OBJECT | Everyday object associated with the suffering | Condition triggered by common activities | Untouched ice water (sensitivity), jar with lid (grip), full coffee (fatigue) |
| EVIDENCE MOMENT | Mirror or close-up showing physical manifestation | Condition has visible symptom | Mirror checking gumline, hairbrush with loss, examining swollen hands |

## **The Cover Test (Non-Negotiable)**

After writing your agitation prompt, apply this test: If someone covered the section headline and only saw the image, could they guess what specific health problem this is about? If NO → your Condition Anchor is too weak or missing.

## **Mobile Readability Rules (CRITICAL)**

**These rules replace all previous diagram text specifications. The old rules (6 labels, 25 words, 48pt) produced text too small to read on mobile. These new rules are non-negotiable.**

**Label Limits**

* Maximum 4 labels (down from 6\)

* Maximum 15 words total across all labels (down from 25\)

* Maximum 5 words per individual label (down from 6\)

* ONE tier of text only — NO sublabels, NO secondary text, NO annotations

**Size & Readability**

* Each label must occupy at least 15% of the image width

* Text must be readable when image is displayed at 375px wide without zooming

* Do NOT use small text, fine print, captions, annotations, or any text smaller than the main labels

* ALL text same size — no hierarchy, no smaller supporting text

* Sans-serif font, high contrast (white/light text on dark, dark text on light)

**Visual Carries Meaning**

If the diagram needs more than 4 labels to be understood, the VISUAL isn't doing its job. Use arrows, colors, X marks, checkmarks, dotted lines, solid lines, red→green transitions. Text only NAMES things — visuals EXPLAIN them.

## **Mandatory Diagram Text Block**

# SALES PAGE IMAGE SOP: Complete Visual Strategy

## OVERVIEW

This SOP generates emotionally resonant images for the 55+ Sales Page Template. Every key section gets visual support that does the emotional heavy lifting.

**Inputs Required:** Strategic Intelligence Brief \+ Completed CONFIG \+ **Product Image** (bottle/jar/box photo or render — MUST be uploaded before generating Hero A, Hero C, mechanism diagram, or alternatives diagram)

## Why This Works

**55+ Reality:** This demographic needs CLARITY. Every image must communicate instantly — no abstract art, no ambiguity.

**The Emotional Arc:**

- HERO → Hope (this could be you)  
- AGITATION → Recognition (that IS me)  
- MECHANISM → Understanding (now I get it)  
- ALTERNATIVES → Validation (I wasn't wrong to fail)  
- TWO PATHS → Decision (which future do I want?)

**Diagram Power:** The UMP/UMS is ABSTRACT. A diagram makes it CONCRETE. "Vitamin D receptors" is words. A diagram showing empty receptors being filled is VISCERAL.

## Visual Standards (ALL Images)

- **Eyes:** Natural ONLY — "soft blue," "warm brown," "gentle grey-green" — NEVER "bright/piercing/vivid/striking"  
- **Expressions:** Dignified emotion — NOT theatrical pain or fake smiles  
- **Demographics:** EXACT match to research  
- **Settings:** CULTURALLY APPROPRIATE to target market  
- **Hands:** Specify "hands naturally at sides" or "hands in lap" to avoid AI artifacts

### Text Rules by Image Type

| Image Type | Text Rule | Governs |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **People images** (hero, agitation, doctor, two paths) | **ABSOLUTE NO TEXT** — no labels, no words, no captions, nothing | All portrait prompts |
| **Mechanism diagrams** (MECH1/MECH2/MECH3) | **LIMITED TEXT ALLOWED** — see rules below | Mechanism section |
| **Alternatives diagrams** (ALT1/ALT2) | **LIMITED TEXT ALLOWED** — see rules below | Alternatives section |

### ⚠️ DIAGRAM TEXT RULES (Non-Negotiable)

Include this block in EVERY diagram prompt:

⚠️ TEXT RULES:

\- Max 6 labels

\- Max 25 words total

\- Max 6 words per label

\- 48pt minimum equivalent

\- Sans-serif font

\- High contrast (white/light text on dark, dark text on light)

\- Passes Thumb Test at 375px (readable on mobile without zooming)

**Why these limits:** Diagrams are viewed on mobile phones by 55+ users. Every extra label is visual noise that kills comprehension. If the diagram needs more than 6 labels to explain the mechanism, the mechanism explanation is too complex — simplify the concept first, then diagram it.

### Diagram Labels: In TARGET LANGUAGE

All diagram text must be in the target market's language. English labels on a Dutch sales page break trust instantly.

## Product Visibility Rules (CRITICAL)

| Image | Product Visible? | Placement | Size |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **HERO** | ✅ YES | Counter/table, natural placement | 15-20% of frame |
| **AGITATION** | ❌ NO | — | — |
| **DOCTOR** | ❌ NO | — | — |
| **MECHANISM DIAGRAM** | ✅ YES | Center/transition zone | Product name or bottle icon |
| **ALTERNATIVES DIAGRAM** | ✅ YES | Solution section | Product name or bottle icon |
| **TWO PATHS — WITHOUT** | ❌ NO | — | — |
| **TWO PATHS — WITH** | ⚠️ OPTIONAL | Counter/background if included | 10-15%, subtle |

### Why These Rules

**HERO — YES:** The hero establishes "this product \= this transformation." Product must be present but NOT dominant. Person is the hero, product is the tool.

**AGITATION — NO:** This is pure pain recognition. Product here feels exploitative. Let them feel the problem BEFORE showing the solution.

**DOCTOR — NO:** Doctor's credibility stands alone. Product in doctor shot feels like paid endorsement (which undermines trust).

**MECHANISM DIAGRAM — YES:** The diagram explains HOW the product works. Product/ingredient must be the intervention point.

**ALTERNATIVES DIAGRAM — YES:** The comparison shows why \[BRAND\] succeeds where others fail. Product name must appear in the "solution" section.

**WITHOUT — NO:** This is the nightmare future. Product here makes no sense narratively.

**WITH — OPTIONAL:** Can work either way:

- WITH product: "This is my secret" — testimonial feel  
- WITHOUT product: Pure aspiration — "This could be you"  
- If included, must be SUBTLE (background, not held)

# PART 5: AGITATION IMAGE (1:1)

**Purpose:** Visualize the pain from Section 6\. Make the problem FELT, not just read.

**🚫 PRODUCT: NO — this is pure pain recognition, no solution yet**

## Agitation Philosophy

This is the "recognition moment" — reader sees themselves. Must be DIGNIFIED struggle, not theatrical suffering. The 55+ audience rejects pity.

**CRITICAL: Every agitation image has TWO LAYERS that work together:**

**Layer 1: The Emotional Moment** — The setting, body language, and private struggle (what A1-A5 define).

**Layer 2: The Condition Anchor** — A specific visual element that ties the emotion to THIS product's specific problem. Without this, the image becomes generic "person feeling bad" that could be about anything.

### The Condition Anchor (MANDATORY for all A-types)

Every agitation prompt MUST include a Condition Anchor — the visual element that makes the image SPECIFIC to the health condition being addressed. Choose one anchor type:

| Anchor Type | What It Is | Use When | Examples |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **BODY FOCUS** | Person's hand, posture, or gaze draws attention to the affected area | The condition has a specific location on the body | Hand on jaw (gums), gripping knee (joints), touching temples (headache), hand through thinning hair (hair loss) |
| **TRIGGER OBJECT** | An everyday object in the scene associated with the specific suffering | The condition is triggered by common activities or objects | Untouched ice water (gum sensitivity), jar with lid (grip/joints), reading glasses pushed aside (vision), full coffee cup that didn't help (fatigue) |
| **EVIDENCE MOMENT** | Close-up or mirror element showing the actual physical manifestation | The condition has a visible symptom that can be shown with dignity | Mirror reflection checking gumline (recession), visible swelling on hands (arthritis), skin texture in mirror (aging), thinning crown visible (hair) |

### ⚠️ THE COVER TEST (Non-Negotiable)

After writing your agitation prompt, apply this test:

**If someone covered the section headline and only saw the image, could they guess what specific health problem this is about?**

If the answer is NO → your Condition Anchor is too weak or missing. A worried woman in a living room could be about ANYTHING. A woman wincing while touching her jaw next to an untouched apple tells you EXACTLY what this is about.

### Condition Anchor by Product Category

| Condition | Best Anchor Type | Specific Element |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Oral/Dental (gums, teeth) | Body Focus or Evidence | Hand at jaw, mirror checking gumline, wince at cold drink |
| Joint/Mobility | Body Focus | Hand on knee/hip, grip on banister, testing weight on leg |
| Skin/Anti-Aging | Evidence | Mirror moment, hand touching face, visible texture |
| Hair Loss/Thinning | Body Focus or Evidence | Hand through hair, hairbrush with visible loss, mirror checking crown |
| Fatigue/Energy/Mood | Trigger Object | Full coffee cup, alarm clock, curtains still closed at midday |
| Cardiovascular/Internal | Trigger Object | Medication bottle, blood pressure monitor, untaken walk |
| Respiratory/Breathing | Body Focus | Hand on chest, inhaler nearby, mouth slightly open |
| Vision/Hearing | Body Focus | Squinting, leaning forward, cupping ear, phone at arm's length |
| Digestive | Trigger Object | Untouched meal, hand on stomach, avoided foods visible |
| Weight/Body | Evidence or Body Focus | Mirror, clothes that no longer fit visible, scale in background |

## Mechanism Diagram Philosophy

The sales page combines UMP and UMS in one section. The diagram should show BOTH:

2. RIGHT/BOTTOM: The SOLUTION mechanism (how product fixes it)

## ⚠️ MANDATORY DIAGRAM PROMPT STRUCTURE (All MECH and ALT types)

Every diagram prompt MUST be built using this three-block method. This prevents text-heavy, unreadable diagrams.

**The problem:** When visual layout instructions and label text are mixed together, the AI generates diagrams with too much text, inconsistent fonts, and labels that fight the illustration. The fix: SEPARATE them while writing, then combine into one prompt.

**IMPORTANT: The three blocks are how you WRITE the prompt — not three separate outputs. The final prompt is ONE single copy-paste block for funnelbuilders to paste into Nanobanan.**

### Writing Method: Build in 3 Blocks, Output as 1 Prompt

**BLOCK 1 — VISUAL LAYOUT (write first):** Describe the composition, anatomy, arrows, colors, indicators (X/✓/⚠).

**ZERO text content in this block.** Don't write label wording here. Only describe what the viewer SEES — shapes, arrows, colors, spatial relationships. Let X marks, checkmarks, dotted/solid arrows, and color carry meaning.

**BLOCK 2 — ⚠️ TEXT LABELS (write second):** A numbered list of the EXACT labels to place on the diagram. Pre-write every word.

Rules:

- Max 6 labels  
- Max 25 words total across all labels  
- Max 6 words per individual label  
- Number each label: `1. "Exact Label Text" — location (X words)`  
- Include running word count total  
- If you can't fit the concept in 25 words → the visual isn't doing enough work. Simplify the concept, strengthen the visual, reduce the text.

**Example (good):**

⚠️ TEXT LABELS (6 labels, 15 words):

1\. "SURFACE TREATMENTS" — header above failed solutions (2 words)

2\. "Miss The Root Cause" — sublabel under failed group (4 words)

3\. "ROOT CAUSE" — pointing to deep tissue (2 words)

4\. "Collagen Depletion" — sublabel under root cause (2 words)

5\. "GUMIVA" — next to golden arrow (1 word)

6\. "Rebuilds Collagen From Within" — under Gumiva (4 words)

TOTAL: 6 labels, 15 words

**Example (bad — DON'T do this):**

Failed Solution 1: "Sensitivity Toothpastes"

→ Label: "Numbs surface only"

Failed Solution 2: "Prescription Mouthwashes"

→ Label: "Kills bacteria, not the cause"

Failed Solution 3: "Gum Graft Surgery"

→ Label: "Patches outside, costly"

↑ Labels mixed into layout instructions. 3 labels just for failed solutions. Explanatory text that should be VISUAL (arrows missing target) is instead written as label text. Total words already over budget before the solution labels.

**BLOCK 3 — ⚠️ TEXT RULES (write last):** The rendering specifications — always the same block:

⚠️ TEXT RULES:

\- 48pt minimum font size

\- Sans-serif font (clean, modern)

\- High contrast throughout

\- Headers largest, sublabels smaller but still 48pt+

\- Passes Thumb Test at 375px mobile width

\- X marks and checkmarks do heavy lifting — text is minimal

### The Principle

**Visuals explain. Labels name. Rules constrain.**

If the diagram needs more than 6 labels to be understood, the VISUAL isn't doing its job. Arrows, colors, X marks, checkmarks, dotted lines, solid lines, red→green transitions — these are your vocabulary. Text is punctuation.

### Output \= ONE Prompt

After writing all three blocks, **combine them into a single continuous prompt** — one block of text that a funnelbuilder pastes into Nanobanan. The block markers (`── BLOCK 1 ──` etc.) in the templates below are there to help you WRITE the prompt correctly. They stay in the final output as section dividers — they don't mean "generate three separate images."
